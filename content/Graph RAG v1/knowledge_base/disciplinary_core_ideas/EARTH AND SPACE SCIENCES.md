# EARTH AND SPACE SCIENCES

## Description

Earth and Space Sciences encompass a critical area of study that evaluates students' knowledge and understanding within the scientific discipline. This field includes the exploration of Earth's systems—such as land, water, atmosphere, and biology—and examines how human activities interact with these systems. It is a vital component of the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) framework, which provides a structured approach to assessing scientific literacy among students.

The NAEP assessment framework identifies Earth and Space Sciences as one of three disciplinary concepts fundamental to the evaluation of student competency in science, specifically incorporating earth sciences and astronomy. This highlights the importance of these subjects in fostering a holistic understanding of the planet and its place in the universe, as well as the complexities involved with human impacts on the Earth's systems.

Overall, Earth and Space Sciences serve as a foundational element in education by informing students about their planet and encouraging critical thinking about environmental and astronomical phenomena, thereby preparing them for informed citizenship and stewardship of the Earth's resources.
