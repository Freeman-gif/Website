# CLIMATE CHANGE

## Description

Climate change is a complex phenomenon characterized by significant alterations in climate patterns and global temperatures over time. This phenomenon is primarily driven by human activities, particularly the emissions of greenhouse gases. These emissions contribute to the warming of the planet, leading to a variety of environmental impacts.

The causes of climate change encompass both natural and anthropogenic factors. Human activities, such as the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, are major contributors to the greenhouse gas emissions that exacerbate climate change. Additionally, there are natural influences that can affect climate, including solar system dynamics, which also play a role in the long-term variations in Earth's climate.

The consequences of climate change are profound and widespread, affecting weather patterns, sea levels, and biodiversity. As global temperatures rise, regions experience altered weather conditions, including more frequent and severe storms, droughts, and heatwaves. These changes not only pose risks to natural ecosystems but also threaten agricultural productivity, water supply, and human health.

Overall, climate change encapsulates a range of changes in environmental conditions, shaped by both human actions and natural processes, leading to significant challenges that require urgent global attention and action.
