# PERFORMANCE TASK

## Description

The term "performance task" refers to a specific type of assessment designed to engage students in extensive and in-depth work over multiple class periods. This approach not only incorporates definitive learning objectives but also emphasizes the application of scientific and engineering practices, thereby creating an interactive learning experience. 

Performance tasks are characterized by their requirement for students to solve open-ended problems, compelling them to apply their knowledge and skills in realistic, real-world contexts. This not only aids in the demonstration of students' understanding of a particular subject—whether it be science, engineering, or another discipline—but also serves as a crucial tool for evaluating educational outcomes. 

The structure of performance tasks supports extended engagement, allowing students to immerse themselves in the problem-solving process while integrating critical thinking and practical application. As such, these tasks play a vital role in fostering deeper learning and understanding, moving beyond traditional testing methods that may only assess rote memorization or superficial knowledge. 

In summary, performance tasks are multifaceted assessments that challenge students to utilize their academic skills in practical scenarios, promoting both learning and evaluation of competencies in a comprehensive manner.
