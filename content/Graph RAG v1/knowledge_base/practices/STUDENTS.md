# STUDENTS

## Description

STUDENTS are individuals engaged in various learning activities within the educational system, particularly focused on science and engineering practices. They participate actively in learning processes that involve analyzing and interpreting data, working with scientific phenomena, and applying models during assessments. As part of their educational experience, students utilize various tools to enhance their understanding and problem-solving skills in science contexts.

Students seek to acquire essential skills and knowledge required for future employability and personal development. Enrolled in educational institutions, they are involved in assessments designed to measure their performance according to standards such as the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS). These assessments serve not only to evaluate their understanding of science concepts but also to deepen their engagement in educational programs.

Moreover, students are known to participate in structured educational assessments like the Maryland assessments and the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) Science Assessment. These evaluations are intended to assess their mastery of scientific principles through practical tasks based on real-world scenarios. The insights gained from these assessments are crucial for understanding students' educational trajectories and for guiding improvements in instructional practices.

Students are also the primary beneficiaries of educational initiatives and the assessment systems in place. It is essential for them to receive clear communication regarding their evaluation results and performance standards. In their learning journeys, they submit work to classroom teachers for feedback and evaluation, contributing to both their educational growth and the overall effectiveness of the assessment strategies employed by educational institutions.

In summary, STUDENTS are a diverse group of individuals who play a vital role in the learning landscape of science and engineering education. They are actively engaged in assessments that enhance their understanding and applicable skills, necessitating a thorough support system to facilitate their educational success and advancement within the STEM fields.
