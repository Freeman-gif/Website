# FORCE

## Description

The entity "FORCE" refers to a fundamental concept in physics, defined as an influence that results in a change in an object's motion. This influence is characterized by both strength and direction, highlighting the vector nature of force. Essentially, a force acts as an interaction that prompts an object to alter its state of motion, which includes changes in speed or direction.

The concept of force is often analyzed through the lens of the net force acting on an object, which is determined by the vector sum of all individual forces applied to that object. The net force governs how the object will move, as it dictates whether the object accelerates, decelerates, or maintains its current state of motion. This principle is central to Newton's laws of motion, which provide a framework for understanding how forces operate in the physical world.

In summary, the entity "FORCE" encapsulates the definition of force as both an influence and an interaction that results in a change in motion. It is characterized by its strength and direction and is fundamentally reliant on the sum of forces acting upon an object to determine the outcome of its motion.
