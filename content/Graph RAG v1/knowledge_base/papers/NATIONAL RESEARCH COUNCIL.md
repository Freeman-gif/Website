# NATIONAL RESEARCH COUNCIL

## Description

The National Research Council (NRC) is an integral component of the National Academy of Sciences, dedicated to advancing science education through expert advice, comprehensive reports, and recommendations aimed at establishing educational standards. The NRC focuses on fostering effective instructional practices, particularly emphasizing the needs of English Language Learners. By providing content standards and promoting effective teaching strategies, the Council aims to enhance educational practices across various contexts.

In addition to its guidance on instruction, the NRC is also responsible for publishing scholarly research that encompasses a wide range of educational topics. This includes developing frameworks for K-12 science education that are mindful of the diverse backgrounds and learning needs of students. The emphasis on considering diverse learners is a crucial aspect of their approach, ensuring that educational frameworks are inclusive and effective for all students.

Overall, the National Research Council serves as a pivotal organization in the landscape of science education, influencing educational policy and practices through its research, recommendations, and advocacy for high-quality education standards.
