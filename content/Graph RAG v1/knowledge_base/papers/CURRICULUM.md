# CURRICULUM

## Description

The term "Curriculum" refers to a comprehensive framework that outlines the educational course structure, content, and standards that guide teaching and assessment practices in schools. It serves as a foundational element in the educational system, shaping the instructional strategies and materials developed for use in classrooms. Curriculum encompasses a wide range of components, including the specific educational materials, learning objectives, and performance expectations designed to align with state assessments.

The curriculum not only details the content to be taught but also influences how teaching and learning are conducted. It establishes clear guidelines for educational performance, which assessments are designed to measure. Through this structured approach, the curriculum aims to ensure that all students have access to a consistent and coherent educational experience, ultimately aligning teaching practices with desired learning outcomes.

Additionally, the curriculum encompasses instructional strategies that educators employ to facilitate learning and engagement in students. By integrating these elements, the curriculum serves as a vital link between educational intentions and student assessments, ensuring that both teaching and assessment practices are effectively coordinated to achieve educational goals.
