# NAEP

## Description

The National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) is an essential assessment organization in the United States that evaluates student performance across various subjects. Established in 1969, NAEP conducts assessments for students in Grades 4, 8, and 12, making it the largest nationally representative assessment of what students in both public and private schools know and can do in multiple subject areas.

NAEP measures academic achievement in a broad range of disciplines, including science, where it evaluates students' knowledge, understanding of principles, and engagement in scientific inquiry. The program aims to provide credible and comprehensive information about educational performance in the U.S. educational system, highlighting how students are performing academically over time.

In its ongoing efforts to modernize assessment methods, NAEP is transitioning towards computer-based testing, ensuring that its evaluations remain relevant and efficient in the digital age. By continuously assessing student performance, NAEP offers valuable insights into educational progress and helps shape educational policies and practices across the nation.

Through its rigorous and standardized testing framework, NAEP serves as a critical tool for understanding the academic capabilities of students in the United States and contributes significantly to discussions on education reform and improvement.
