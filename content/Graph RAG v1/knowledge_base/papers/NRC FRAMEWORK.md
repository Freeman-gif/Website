# NRC FRAMEWORK

## Description

The NRC Framework for K–12 Science Education serves as a foundational guide for science learning and assessment in schools. Developed by the National Research Council, it outlines a comprehensive set of guidelines and expectations designed to improve science education across various grade levels. 

Central to the NRC Framework is the emphasis on the interconnections between core scientific concepts and skills. As students progress through their educational journey, the framework encourages revisiting and deepening their understanding of these concepts. This iterative approach is vital for fostering a robust comprehension of scientific principles, which are essential for both academic success and real-world applications.

Furthermore, the NRC Framework delineates distinct practices and goals for science and engineering learning, aiming to structure educational experiences that are coherent and connected. It provides a well-structured pathway for teachers and educators, suggesting specific content and assessment practices that promote effective science learning.

Within the framework, there is a strong focus on performance expectations—an essential component that guides educators in assessing students’ understanding of scientific concepts. By adhering to these standards, educators can ensure that students are not only absorbing information but are also equipped to apply their knowledge in practical situations.

Overall, the NRC Framework for K–12 Science Education is a pivotal instrument in shaping modern science education, emphasizing a cohesive structure that bridges grades and enhances the learning experience through a focus on both content and skills development in the realm of science and engineering.
