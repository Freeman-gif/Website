# OECD

## Description

The OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) is an intergovernmental economic organization dedicated to promoting policies that enhance the economic and social well-being of people globally. It serves as a platform where governments can collaborate to share ideas, experiences, and best practices aimed at fostering economic growth and improving living standards.

One of the key initiatives undertaken by the OECD is the administration of the PISA (Programme for International Student Assessment) science test. This assessment evaluates the scientific competencies of students from various countries, providing valuable data that allows for comparisons of educational outcomes on an international scale. PISA aims to assess how well students can apply their knowledge and skills in science to real-life contexts, thus serving as a critical tool for understanding and improving the effectiveness of education systems worldwide.

In addition to conducting the PISA assessments, the OECD provides guidelines and assessment frameworks for education. These frameworks are designed to assist countries in developing their educational policies, guiding them in measuring and enhancing the quality of education. Through its comprehensive approach, the OECD not only evaluates educational performance but also encourages the adoption of effective teaching and learning practices based on robust research and analysis.

Overall, the OECD plays a pivotal role in shaping economic policies, evaluating educational outcomes, and providing frameworks that support the continuous improvement of education systems across its member and partner countries, thereby contributing significantly to the global understanding of what fosters social and economic development.
