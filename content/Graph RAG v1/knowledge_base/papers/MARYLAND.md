# MARYLAND

## Description

Maryland is a U.S. state recognized for its contributions to educational systems, particularly through the Maryland State Performance Assessment System (MSPAP), which was in operation from 1991 to 2002. This system was a pivotal part of Maryland's approach to educational accountability and reform, aiming to enhance the quality of education by implementing performance-based assessment programs. These programs were integral in evaluating the state's educational standards and ensuring that students met specified learning outcomes. By using MSPAP, Maryland focused on assessing the performance of students in a manner that emphasized comprehension and application of knowledge, rather than rote memorization. As a result, the state has become a significant player in the landscape of educational reform, showcasing how standardized assessment can influence curriculum development and teaching methods within schools.
