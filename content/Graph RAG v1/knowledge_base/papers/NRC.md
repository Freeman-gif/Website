# NRC

## Description

The National Research Council (NRC) plays a pivotal role in shaping K–12 education, particularly in the field of science. It is recognized for developing comprehensive frameworks that guide science education, laying a foundation for innovative teaching and learning practices. One of the NRC's significant contributions is the creation of a vision for science education that has been instrumental in the establishment of the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS). These standards reflect a contemporary approach to science education, aiming to enhance students' understanding and engagement in scientific concepts, as well as to promote critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Through its efforts, the NRC has set forth guidelines that influence curriculum development and instructional strategies within the K–12 education system. The frameworks provided by the NRC serve as valuable resources for educators, policymakers, and curriculum developers, facilitating the implementation of effective science education across diverse educational settings. By aligning educational practices with the NGSS, the NRC ensures that students receive a coherent and relevant science education that equips them for future academic and career opportunities in science and related fields.
