# ELLS

## Description

English Language Learners, commonly referred to as ELLs, are students whose primary language is not English and who are currently in the process of developing their English proficiency. These learners require tailored instructional support to effectively participate in academic settings and to navigate the complexities of learning content across various subjects while acquiring the English language.

ELLs need to enhance their English skills to facilitate their understanding of academic content. This entails both language development and content mastery, necessitating specific instructional strategies that integrate language learning with content learning. Teachers and educational programs often implement specialized approaches designed to support ELLs in their dual tasks of improving their language abilities and grasping subject matter effectively.

In summary, ELLs are a unique group of learners in educational environments who face the challenge of mastering English while also engaging with various academic subjects. Their successful integration into the educational framework is reliant on targeted support and instructional strategies that acknowledge their linguistic needs and assist them in becoming proficient English speakers.
