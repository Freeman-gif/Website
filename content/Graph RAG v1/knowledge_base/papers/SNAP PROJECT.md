# SNAP PROJECT

## Description

The SNAP Project is a state-supported initiative designed to enhance assessment systems within educational environments, specifically targeting the improvement of student performance assessments in science. Its primary focus is on developing assessments aligned with the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS), which promotes high-quality science education, particularly in early elementary grades. The project seeks to address the systemic challenges in assessment practices by evaluating the implementation and reliability of performance assessments that contribute to school accountability.

By exploring the implications of computerization in assessments, the SNAP Project aims to innovate instructional strategies that drive curriculum improvements across various grade levels. It emphasizes formative evaluation, providing educators and districts with timely data that informs instructional decisions, leading to improved teaching quality. This includes the development of model tasks for in-class performance assessments, ensuring these assessments are adaptable to meet educational standards while supporting diverse learning contexts.

The SNAP Project also entails a comprehensive evaluation of students’ scientific reasoning and understanding, aiming to systematize assessments that reflect students' capabilities accurately. Additionally, the initiative is linked to federally mandated testing protocols and is part of the California Science Assessment system, which emphasizes the necessity of integrating external mandated testing components in educational evaluations.

Through these endeavors, the SNAP Project not only enhances assessment methods but also strives to ensure that students have access to quality learning experiences. By prioritizing the creation of open-access educational resources and innovative assessment methodologies, the project is positioned as a pivotal player in transforming educational assessment practices, facilitating accountability, and enhancing student learning outcomes across California.
