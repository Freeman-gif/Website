# NO CHILD LEFT BEHIND

## Description

The "No Child Left Behind" (NCLB) is a federal education policy established in 2001, aimed at enhancing accountability within the educational system and improving student performance assessments. This act represents a significant legislative effort to ensure that all students, regardless of their backgrounds, achieve proficiency in essential academic skills, particularly in language arts and mathematics.

Key components of the NCLB include the requirement for standardized testing across multiple grade levels. The act mandates that states develop and implement assessments to evaluate student progress in these core subjects, thereby fostering a culture of accountability among schools and educators. The underlying goal of the policy is to ensure that no student is overlooked in the educational process, hence the title "No Child Left Behind."

Overall, the NCLB act symbolizes a national commitment to educational reform and aims to frequently assess educational outcomes to ensure that every child has the opportunity to succeed academically. The federal initiative emphasizes skill acquisition in fundamental areas while promoting transparency and improvement within the school systems across the United States.
