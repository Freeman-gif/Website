# NATURAL SELECTION

## Description

Natural selection is a fundamental biological process through which certain traits within a population become more or less prevalent. This phenomenon occurs as a result of the advantages these traits confer upon individuals in terms of survival and reproduction within a specific environment. The process of natural selection drives the evolution of species over time, as it systematically favors traits that enhance an organism's ability to thrive and adapt to varying environmental conditions. Consequently, natural selection plays a crucial role in shaping the diversity of life on Earth, influencing the characteristics of populations as they respond to changes in their surroundings. Through natural selection, advantageous traits that improve fitness are passed down to successive generations, leading to gradual changes in species and promoting ongoing adaptation in the face of environmental challenges.
