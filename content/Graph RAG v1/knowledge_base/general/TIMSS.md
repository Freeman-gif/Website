# TIMSS

## Description

The TIMSS international tests assess students’ knowledge of science, applied knowledge, and reasoning scientifically.

## Related Concepts

- [[STUDENTS]] — Students are the participants who are assessed in the TIMSS tests to measure their science knowledge and skills.
- [[BOSTON]] — TIMSS assessments are conducted by the TIMSS and PIRLS International Study Center located in Boston
- [[PISA]] — PISA and TIMSS are both significant international assessments that evaluate student performance in key subjects across countries.
- [[OECD]] — The OECD oversees the TIMSS tests, providing a framework and guidelines for international assessments.
- [[OECD]] — The OECD oversees and reports on the results and methodologies of the TIMSS assessments
- [[L. R. JONES]] — L. R. Jones contributed to the development of the TIMSS 2015 Assessment Framework
- [[G. WHEELER]] — G. Wheeler contributed to the development of the TIMSS 2015 Assessment Framework
- [[NGSS]] — The TIMSS tests contribute to meeting the requirements set by NGSS in science education standards.
- [[HIGHER ORDER SKILLS]] — The TIMSS tests focus on assessing higher order skills to prepare students for future employment in society.
- [[VICTORIA A.S. CENTURINO]] — Victoria A.S. Centurino is associated with the TIMSS assessment framework
