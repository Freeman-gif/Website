# INNOVATIVE LAB NETWORK

## Description

The Innovative Lab Network (ILN) is a collaborative consortium comprising 18 states, focusing on advancements in educational assessments and the development of resource banks. This network is dedicated to enhancing science education by harnessing collective expertise and sharing resources across its member states. 

Spearheaded by the Council of Chief State School Officers (CCSSO) and supported by the Stanford Center for Assessment, Learning, and Equity (SCALE), the ILN aims to create a comprehensive task and resource bank. This initiative seeks to provide educators with effective tools and strategies to improve the quality of science education, ensuring that schools can deliver consistent and high-quality instructional materials.

By working together, the states in the ILN leverage their diverse experiences and best practices to foster innovation in educational assessments. This collaborative approach not only enhances resource accessibility for educators but also promotes the sharing of successful methodologies that can be adapted or scaled across various educational contexts. Overall, the Innovative Lab Network plays a vital role in supporting science education through its emphasis on collaboration, resource development, and shared learning among its member states.
