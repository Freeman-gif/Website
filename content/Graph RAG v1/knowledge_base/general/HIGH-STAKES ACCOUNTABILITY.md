# HIGH-STAKES ACCOUNTABILITY

## Description

High-stakes accountability refers to the practice of holding schools and educators accountable for their students' performance on standardized tests, often using these results for significant decisions.

## Related Concepts

- [[POLICYMAKERS]] — Policymakers are involved in creating high-stakes accountability measures that affect educational assessment programs.
- [[SCHOOL ADMINISTRATORS]] — School administrators must adapt to high-stakes accountability requirements when managing school processes and assessment outcomes.
