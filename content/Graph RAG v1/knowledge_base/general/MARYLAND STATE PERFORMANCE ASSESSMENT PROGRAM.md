# MARYLAND STATE PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT PROGRAM

## Description

The Maryland State Performance Assessment Program (MSPAP) was an educational assessment initiative designed to evaluate student performance across the state of Maryland from its inception in 1991 until its conclusion in 2002. This program was unique in its approach, as it focused entirely on performance-based assessments rather than traditional standardized testing methods. MSPAP utilized interdisciplinary performance activities and extended, multi-part tasks, which allowed for a more comprehensive evaluation of students' skills and knowledge in various subject areas.

The primary goal of MSPAP was to provide a holistic view of student learning and school performance. By incorporating multiple aspects of student work, the program aimed to measure not only what students knew but also their ability to apply their knowledge in practical and real-world contexts. This approach was intended to promote deeper learning and understanding among students, encouraging the development of critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Over its operational years, MSPAP played a significant role in shaping educational assessments in Maryland, aligning with broader educational reforms aimed at improving teaching and learning outcomes. By focusing on performance-based assessments, MSPAP contributed to the dialogue on effective evaluation methods within the educational community, providing insights into student capabilities that traditional assessments may not have captured.

Overall, the Maryland State Performance Assessment Program served as a pioneering effort in student assessment during its time, influencing subsequent assessment strategies and policies in Maryland and potentially beyond.
